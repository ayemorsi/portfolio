Amazon

I worked on a three-month project to design a safety reporting application for Amazon employees. The goal was to replace the manual, paper-based process with a quick and easy digital solution. This required maintaining compatibility with the existing backend and simplifying the complex hazard classification process for less experienced users.

I conducted usability testing with 30 participants and iterated based on feedback, improving reporting accuracy from 51% to 93%. I collaborated with a UX writer to simplify hazard terminology and created clear descriptions to guide users. The final design allowed employees to submit safety concerns efficiently while maintaining data integrity.

ZoomScript

In 2016, I worked on ZoomScript, a modern application designed to simplify prescription management and refills for patients. Over four weeks, I conducted market research, competitive analysis, and usability testing to understand patient pain points. Insights from 15 user interviews and surveys revealed that 86% of participants found traditional refill methods cumbersome and slow.

As a UX/UI designer and researcher, I crafted low-fi to high-fi prototypes using tools like Adobe XD and Photoshop. The result was an intuitive mobile solution enabling patients to manage and refill prescriptions conveniently, addressing the need for a more seamless pharmacy experience.
